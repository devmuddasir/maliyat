Overview of Financial Due Diligence

Financial due diligence provides an objective assessment of a target company's financial health, enabling informed investment decisions. Our services cover financial statement analysis, cash flow and debt analysis, and risk identification.

Financial Statement Review

Comprehensive analysis of income statements, balance sheets, and cash flows to identify trends and assess financial stability.

Quality of Earnings

Examination of revenue sources, one-time expenses, and accounting practices to ensure sustainable earnings levels.

Cash Flow Analysis

Assessment of cash inflows and outflows to gauge liquidity and determine if the company generates adequate free cash flow.

Key Components of Financial Due Diligence

Debt & Capital Structure

Analyzing the company's debt load, interest coverage, and capital structure for potential leverage risks.

Working Capital Review

Examining accounts receivable, inventory, and accounts payable for efficient working capital management.

Revenue & Profitability

Assessing revenue drivers, profit margins, and cost structure to determine profitability sustainability.

Risk Evaluation

Identifying operational, market, and financial risks that may impact the business and its valuation.
